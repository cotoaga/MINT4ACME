Vision & Concept

This document describes our vision and concept for an integrated internet based multimedia solution for the automotive industry. At the bottom of this document you can find hints on how to use the explorer (left hand side) and the collection browser (bottom).

MINT (Multimedia Information, Negotiation and Transaction) represents a revolutionary approach to automotive sales and customer engagement. This framework integrates multimedia presentation, product configuration, and sales support into a cohesive platform that bridges the gap between manufacturers, dealerships, and customers.

Our Vision

We envision a future where automotive sales transcend the physical limitations of showrooms, creating immersive digital experiences that engage customers wherever they are. MINT will transform how vehicles are presented, configured, and sold, while seamlessly integrating with existing business systems.

Key elements of our vision include:

  • Unified Customer Experience: Providing consistent, high-quality vehicle information and configuration tools across all touchpoints, from dealership to web.
  • Real-time Information Access: Ensuring that sales staff and customers always have access to current product specifications, pricing, and availability.
  • Multimedia Richness: Leveraging advances in web technologies to create compelling presentations that showcase vehicles effectively.
  • Seamless Integration: Connecting dealership systems with manufacturer databases to streamline the sales process.
  • Offline Capability: Delivering full functionality even in environments with limited or no connectivity.

Conceptual Framework

MINT is structured around a four-layer architecture that separates concerns while providing maximum flexibility:

  1. Presentation Layer: The user interface components that deliver multimedia content and interactive features.
  2. Business Logic Layer: The core functionality that drives the application, including configuration rules and pricing calculations.
  3. Data Access Layer: The mechanisms for storing, retrieving, and synchronizing information across systems.
  4. Infrastructure Layer: The foundational technologies that enable integration with existing systems and networks.

This layered approach allows MINT to adapt to changing business requirements and technological capabilities, ensuring that the framework remains relevant and valuable over time.

Navigation Guide

The MINT presentation includes two primary navigation tools:

  • Explorer (Left Side): Provides a hierarchical view of all content in the presentation. Click on folders to expand or collapse sections, and click on documents to view their content in the main frame.
  • Perspective Selector (Top Right): Allows you to switch between different views of the content, including:
    • Complete: Shows all available content
    • Business: Focuses on business aspects and benefits
    • Technology: Highlights technical implementation details
    • Quick: Provides a streamlined overview of key points

Additionally, you can use the navigation buttons in each content page to move sequentially through the presentation.

Digital Archaeology Note: This is a modern restoration of a 1997 web application. The original used Flash animations, auto-playing WAV files, frames, and obsolete web technologies. These have been replaced with HTML5 equivalents while preserving the original look and navigation structure. Background images are now more visible, and redundant copyright footers have been removed.
