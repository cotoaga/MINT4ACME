MINT will be highly graphical in concept: the application will be capable of displaying all kinds of files that are supported in a browser environment. This will naturally evolve with time, as HTML evolves itselfe, effectively giving ACME a self-evolving application at no extra cost.

Initially supported file formats will be:

  • GIF and JPEG for pictures
  • MPEG, AVI and QuickTime for video
  • Virtual reality worlds built with VRML
  • Wave, MIDI and different sound streams

MINT will consist of functionality pages (little text, many graphics, functionality controls) and help pages (much text, many how-to wizards and controls). The Help pages will be capable of being accessed directly (as a manual), as in-context Help, and automatically in case of repeated errors.

MINT will be capable of supporting and integrating the following vehicle sales processes. The Chapter Technical Environment shows for some of the following processes examples how the different MINT versions will support these processes at different sites:

  • The assisted sales process at the sales location, where the customer visits the dealership in order to investigate and possibly buy a car; at the dealership he/she interacts with a sales representative in what is the current classical sales process.
  • The assisted sales process at the customer location, where the customer is visited by a sales representative at his/her home, office or other convenient location for the negotiation phase; the sales representative carries information about the car or car range and may physically drive a specimen car to the customer.
  • The unassisted sales process at the information location, where the customer can retrieve a large amount of information about a car or car family and receive a quote. This may happen outside or inside a sales location, depending on where the information location (kiosk) is placed. When the kiosk is located inside a sales location, this process may transform itself into the assisted sales process at the sales location.
  • The unassisted sales process at the customer location, where the customer can, by using his/her PC, retrieve a large amount of information about a car or car family and receive a quote. He should also be capable, by using his own equipment to electronically access a ACME or dealer-owned information pool, of obtaining a non-binding delivery date for a particular car configuration.

These four aspects share sub-processes, but require carefully thought-out implementation because of the physical location of the supporting IT infrastructure. We believe that the approach we have taken allows the requested multimedia application to be developed in such a way as to make the physical location of the supporting IT infrastructure transparent, and will therefore fully support all of the above aspects of the sales process.

MINT will normally start at a page giving a high-level picture of available functions:

  • Choice of desired vehicle by family/model
  • Choice of desired vehicle(s) by price ceiling
  • Choice of desired vehicle(s) by function (sports, estate etc.)
  • Resumption of a saved sales process
  • Launch of other available ACME or dealer applications

MINT will not be just a menu system and an integrator of other applications; existing applications within the dealership will be utilized fully for the information content and functionality they may provide, but this will be done as much as possible under the direction of MINT. As an example, MINT will be capable of dynamically configuring a vehicle on-screen in the right power train, colour, trim level and accessories from on-screen pictures of the available features, but the existing configuration validation systems will be the only source of information. The dynamic configuration will reflect the changes in choice made by the customer and/or salesman on the screen:

  • Colour and trim changes will be fully reflected on the screen through the use of special software. The effect will be to show the car on screen in the selected colour and to allow the customer and/or salesman to rotate it through the use of surround video techniques in order to simulate the effect of walking around the car or of turning around inside the car.
  • ACME options and accessories will be shown both in graphical form for selection and, after selection, mounted on the car if they are visible externally or internally; for example, if a customer were to choose an optional CD player, the in-the-car surround video picture would change the standard-mounted radio to the selected one.

All configuration changes will be priced and a running total of the current price will be displayed in a frame on-screen. The total will reflect any net pricing and/or percentage discounts that may be input by the salesman.

The surround video pictures involved in the configuration process will contain intelligent hot spots capable of acting as functionality controls so that they may demonstrate features of the vehicle that are either normally not seen or rarely seen (air bags etc.).

Buttons will be located on-screen to demonstrate other features that may be of interest to the customer, like general safety, economy and performance. MINT can be extended to utilize some over-the-counter applications to promote understanding in the customer of the real meaning of economy and performance:

  • It is possible to simulate a journey that the customer normally performs so that fuel consumption is associated with a real-life journey in a customer's mind. The customer can choose a realistic start and end for a journey, and preferential speeds. These parameters would be applied the chosen car's fuel consumption parameters and realistic geographic information. The results could be compared to those obtained from competitor's models.
  • It is possible to show performance on a racing circuit by using simulator games to give the customer an better idea of acceleration road holding possibilities.

The application will maintain a list of the page addresses (URLs) that are accesses and the order in which they are accessed; this list will be accumulated by MINT, and will be processed at the logical end of a transaction so that unnecessary or repetitive data is eliminated. The processed list will be saved against a customer's name or system-assigned key if the customerís name is not known. The information will be available for further analysis by ACME or to continue a sales process that was interrupted for any reason.
