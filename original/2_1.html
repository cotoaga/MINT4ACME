The development of a solution like the MINT-Application requires very careful consideration of three main issues:

User Interface
The application is going to be used by ACME dealers of varying sizes in many countries, with the implied language, cultural and working habit differences. As a consequence the man-machine interface must be capable of supporting high levels of intuition, but still be within the capabilities of the specified hardware configurations.
User Constituency
Car salesmen are notoriously reluctant to employ sales aids unless they can see a clear advantage to themselves in the support of the selling process, and still see information as power. MINT must clearly supply to them features that are seen as beneficial while at the same time informing the customer without seeming to detract from the position of the salesman as supplier of information.
Productization
MINT is going to be distributed to a high number of ACME dealers who will confidently expect from MINT the same level of quality that they are accustomed to in shrink-wrapped packages. As a consequence, the development of MINT should not be treated as a normal development of a bespoke application, but instead as the development of a commercial product.
